Do you believe the Bible is the Word of God?

Yes. I hold Scripture as divinely inspired and authoritative, while taking its genres, history, and translation seriously.

scriptureauthority Updated May 3, 2026

Why this matters

How a person answers this question shapes everything else they say about God. Some people worry I’ll undercut the Bible by appealing to “translation problems” or other scripture. Others worry the opposite, that I’ll claim a kind of certainty about the text that the text itself doesn’t claim. I want to answer in a way that takes the Bible seriously, the way the Bible presents itself.

Where I stand

I believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God. The Holy Spirit moved through real human authors in real historical settings to give us writings that are trustworthy and authoritative for faith and life. Taking it seriously also means taking its history and the work of its translators seriously.

The reasoning

  1. Scripture testifies of its own inspiration. “All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness” (2 Tim 3:16). Peter says prophecy came “as [men] were moved by the Holy Ghost” (2 Pet 1:21). Inspiration is the Bible’s own claim about itself.
  2. Genre matters. The Bible is a library, not a single document. Poetry isn’t read like history; apocalyptic isn’t read like a letter; wisdom isn’t read like law. I try to read each book the way it presents itself. Anything else flattens what God actually gave us.
  3. History matters. I try to understand what the original authors meant to communicate to their original audience before I ask what it means for me today. The Spirit who inspired Paul to write to the Corinthians is the same Spirit who teaches me through that letter now, but I have to go through what it meant to them first.
  4. Jesus is the center. I read the whole Bible, Old Testament and New, as pointing to and finding its fulfillment in the person and work of Jesus (Luke 24:27, 44; John 5:39).
  5. Translation is real and it isn’t a dodge. Saying that the Bible has come to us through translators isn’t an attack on its authority. It’s a plain fact about how we got the book in our hands. I trust that God has preserved His word well enough to do what He intends through it, and I take seriously the work of careful translation and study.

What I hold firmly / loosely

  • Firmly: The Bible is the inspired Word of God; it is authoritative for faith and life; it points to Jesus.
  • Loosely: Every hard passage figured out, every translation question settled, every disputed reading resolved. I expect to keep learning, and I’d rather walk with someone through a hard text than pretend I’ve solved it.
