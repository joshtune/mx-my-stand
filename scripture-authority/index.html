Do you believe the Bible is the Word of God?

Yes — I hold Scripture as divinely inspired and authoritative, while taking its genres, history, and translation seriously.

scriptureauthority Updated May 1, 2026

Where I stand

I believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God — that the Holy Spirit moved through real human authors in real historical settings to give us writings that are trustworthy and authoritative for faith and life.

How I read it

  • Genre matters. The Bible is a library, not a single document. Poetry isn’t read like history; apocalyptic isn’t read like a letter. I try to read each book the way it presents itself.
  • History matters. I try to understand what the original authors meant to communicate to their original audience before I ask what it means for me today.
  • Jesus is the center. I read the whole Bible — Old Testament and New — as pointing to and finding its fulfillment in the person and work of Jesus.

What I’m not claiming

I’m not claiming I have every hard passage figured out, or that my reading is the only faithful one. I expect to keep learning. But I’m not approaching Scripture as a museum piece or a self-help book — I’m approaching it as a living word from a real God.
