How do you know the Book of Mormon is true?

Because I asked the Lord with an open mind on the validity of its origin, and He answered. That witness is the foundation under every other position on this site.

book-of-mormontestimonyfoundation Updated May 3, 2026

Why this matters

This is the question every other entry on this site stands on. If someone wants to know why I’m a Latter-day Saint, why I trust the Book of Mormon as scripture, why I believe the Church was restored through Joseph Smith, why I land where I land on any of the smaller questions, the honest answer doesn’t start with arguments. It starts here. The honest answer is a kind of knowledge the Bible itself promises to anyone who will ask for it in faith.

Where I stand

I know the Book of Mormon is the word of God. I came to know it the same way the scriptures themselves invite anyone to come to know it. I read and studied it with an open mind, not to compete with other churches but on the validity of its origin, and I asked the Lord in sincere prayer. He answered. From that witness, the rest of what I believe follows: that Joseph Smith was a prophet, and that the Church of Jesus Christ has been restored through him by the Lord Himself. Every article on this site sits on top of that foundation.

The reasoning

This section is not a logical proof of the Book of Mormon. It is the path the scriptures themselves lay out for anyone who wants to know, and the path I walked to receive a witness.

  1. The Bible’s invitation. “If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him. But let him ask in faith, nothing wavering” (James 1:5-6). This is not a Latter-day Saint claim layered over Christianity. It is the Bible’s own pattern. God answers those who ask sincerely. Joseph Smith was fourteen years old when he read that verse and took it at its word. The verse is still there for anyone who will do the same.
  2. The Book of Mormon’s invitation. “I would exhort you that ye would ask God, the Eternal Father, in the name of Christ, if these things are not true; and if ye shall ask with a sincere heart, with real intent, having faith in Christ, he will manifest the truth of it unto you, by the power of the Holy Ghost” (Moroni 10:4). The Book of Mormon makes the same promise about itself that the Bible makes about wisdom in general. It is the same God and the same pattern.
  3. The kind of knowing offered. Spiritual things are not learned the way historical or scientific facts are learned. “I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost, which shall come upon you and which shall dwell in your heart” (D&C 8:2). Paul says the same in different words: “the natural man receiveth not the things of the Spirit of God… because they are spiritually discerned” (1 Cor 2:14). The witness comes directly.
  4. The condition: an honest question. “If any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine, whether it be of God” (John 7:17). The honest seeker has to set down the question “how does this compare to other churches?” and pick up the question “is this true?” James says “ask in faith, nothing wavering”; Moroni says “with a sincere heart, with real intent.”
  5. What follows. If the Book of Mormon is what it claims to be, then Joseph Smith’s account of how it came to him is what it claims to be, and the Church restored through him is what it claims to be. That is not a logical chain I built up to a conclusion. The cornerstone is the witness, and the articles on this site are the explaining I do afterward.
